THE MAMMALIAN HEART

The heart is a pumping organ in the circulatory system which is involved in the pumping of blood round the body. The heart is a cone-shaped structure which is found in the chest or thoracic cavity where it is protected by a bony case called pericardium. It is divided into four chambers which are the left and right auricles and the left and right ventricles. The auricles receive blood while the ventricles pump out blood.

The auricles are separated from the ventricles like valves to prevent the backflow of blood. The opening and closing of these valves is controlled by muscles called heart strings(chordae tendinae. The heart is divided longitudinally into two parts (right and left) by a structure called septum.

Deoxygenated blood flows through the right side of the heart while oxygenated blood flows through the left side of the heart. Note that the walls of the ventricles is 3 times thicker than the walls of the auricles.

BLOOD VESSELS

These are structures that help to convey blood. They are three types: veins, arteries and capillaries.

The veins carry deoxygenated blood to the heart from the body with the exception of the pulmonary vein which carries oxygenated blood from the lungs to the heart. Important veins of the body include the following:

  1. Jugular Veins: It carries deoxygenated blood from the head and the neck.
  2. Subclavian Veins: It carries deoxygenated blood from the hand and the chest to the heart.
  3. Renal Veins: It carries deoxygenated blood from the kidney to the heart.
  4. Hepatic Veins: It carries deoxygenated blood from the liver to the heart.
  5. Hepatic Portal Vein: This vein is unique in that it carries digested food materials from the small intestine to the heart.
  6. Femoral Veins: It carries deoxygenated blood from the thighs to the heart.
  7. Common Iliac Veins: It carries deoxygenated blood from the toes to the heart.
